How to Bench Press

Everything you need to know to safely and effectively perform a bench press

Targeted Muscle Groups

  • Pectoralis
  • Anterior Deltoid
  • Tricep Lateral Head
  • Tricep Medial Head

Walk into any gym in the world, regardless of its size, and you’ll find they all have one thing in common: there will be a bench positioned underneath a secured barbell.

This is because the bench press is a compound movement that targets many of the major muscle groups in the upper body and can be easily overloaded to facilitate progression.

Being one of the most common movements performed in a gym however, it is very often performed incorrectly, inhibiting growth and leading to injury.

Let's go through the basics of performing a barbell bench press


  1. Lie flat on your back on a bench.
  2. Grip the bar with hands just wider than shoulder-width apart, so when you’re at the bottom of your move your hands are directly above your elbows. This allows for maximum force generation.
  3. Retract your shoulder blades, almost as if you're trying to rip your shirt off. This will significantly reduce your chance of shoulder injury, removing it as a main driver in the movement.
  4. Bring the bar slowly down to your chest as you breathe in.
  5. Push up as you breathe out, gripping the bar hard and watching a spot on the ceiling rather than the bar, so you can ensure it travels the same path every time.
